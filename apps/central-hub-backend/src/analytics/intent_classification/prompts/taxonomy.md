# INTENT TAXONOMY LAYER
Below is the complete list of supported intent categories and their descriptions. The primary intent must be chosen from this list:

1. **Course Inquiry**: Queries about class details, curricula, syllabus, training sessions, or schedule details of courses.
2. **Admissions**: Inquiries about application procedures, requirements, eligibility, deadlines, or admissions processing status.
3. **Pricing**: Questions about costs, fees, subscription prices, payment plans, discounts, or requesting quotes.
4. **Registration**: Inquiries or issues regarding signing up, enrolling, registration forms, or portal registrations.
5. **Billing**: Queries about invoices, charges, payment options, receipts, refunds, renewals, or transaction failures.
6. **Technical Support**: Technical troubleshooting, server/widget downtime, login failures, error messages, or integration bugs.
7. **Product Information**: Questions about features, specifications, product updates, SDK capabilities, or API integrations.
8. **Documentation**: Requests for tutorials, installation guidelines, READMEs, reference documentation, or system manuals.
9. **Feature Request**: Requests for new capabilities, layout improvements, enhancements, or future integrations.
10. **Bug Report**: Explicit issues about broken features, crashes, unexpected UI bugs, or reproducible errors.
11. **Complaint**: Expressions of anger, dissatisfaction, delays in support, or issues with product/service quality.
12. **Feedback**: General comments, testimonials, constructive advice, or positive/negative reviews.
13. **Sales Inquiry**: General sales interest, request for general product demonstrations, or talking to sales team members.
14. **Enterprise Inquiry**: High-volume usage, multi-tenant dedicated servers, custom contracts, SLAs, or bulk custom requirements.
15. **General Information**: Standard office hours, physical locations, general company history, small talk, or greetings.
16. **Other**: Fallback category for conversations that are empty, only contain initial greetings, are highly ambiguous, or do not fit any of the above.
